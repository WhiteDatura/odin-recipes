Pizza Margherita

Recipe and steps for a crunchy Pizza alla "Marinara"

Pizza Marinara is a classic of Italian cuisine and one of the simplest versions of pizza. The name "marinara" means "sailor style" and indicates that it was usually enjoyed by fishermen and sailors who could prepare it on board with simple ingredients. Pizza marinara has a tomato sauce as its base, seasoned with garlic, oregano, olive oil and salt. The combination of simple but high quality ingredients creates a wonderful taste that reflects the soul of Italian cuisine.

INGREDIENTS LIST

Dough

  • 470 pastry flour (soft / patent)
  • 30 g durum wheat semolina
  • 300 ml water lukewarm
  • 35 ml olive oil extra virgin
  • 10 g salt
  • 5 g fresh yeast

Sauce

  • 500 g San Marzano tomatoes passed
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper

Topping

  • garlic to taste
  • basil to taste
  • olive oil extra virgin to taste

STEPS

  1. Add the yeast to the lukewarm water and dissolve
  2. Put the flour, durum wheat semolina and salt in the mixing bowl of the stand mixer and let knead with the dough hook on the lowest speed.
  3. Once the dough has a nice smooth surface and there are no more dough scraps hanging from the mixing bowl, turn off the machine.
  4. Release the dough from the hook, shape it into a ball and return it to the bowl.
  5. Cover with a cloth and let rest for at least 2 hours.
  6. Put the passed tomatoes, salt, pepper and oregano in a bowl and mix thoroughly.
  7. Preheat the oven to 250째C (500째F) top/bottom heat.
  8. Peel the garlic and chop it into small pieces.
  9. Pour some of the tomato sauce on the pizza dough and top with the garlic.
  10. Then bake for about 10 minutes at 250째C (500째F) top/bottom heat on the lowest rack. Finally, garnish with the fresh basil leaves, drizzle with olive oil and serve hot.
